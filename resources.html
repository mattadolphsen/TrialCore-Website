Security & Compliance

Last Updated: June 16, 2026

At TrialCore, protecting research data, participant information, and study operations is a foundational principle of our platform design. This statement describes TrialCore's approach to security, privacy, research data management, and healthcare-related information. It is intended for prospective customers, research organizations, sponsors, contract research organizations (CROs), investigators, and other stakeholders evaluating TrialCore.

Our Commitment

  • Clinical research depends on accurate, trustworthy, and well-documented data.
  • TrialCore is designed to support research teams through tools for participant management, visit scheduling and tracking, protocol compliance monitoring, adverse event documentation, protocol deviation management, audit logging, study reporting, and operational oversight.
  • We recognize that research organizations operate in highly regulated environments and require technology that supports accountability, transparency, and data integrity.

Research Data Ownership

  • Research organizations maintain ownership and control of their study data.
  • TrialCore does not claim ownership of customer research data, participant information, study documentation, or protocol-related records entered into the platform.
  • Customers remain responsible for data accuracy, regulatory submissions, participant protections, study oversight, and compliance with applicable regulations and sponsor requirements.

HIPAA Considerations

  • TrialCore may be used by organizations that handle Protected Health Information (PHI) as defined by the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA).
  • Whether HIPAA applies to a particular implementation depends on the type of organization using TrialCore, the data stored within the platform, the contractual relationship between the parties, and applicable laws and regulations.
  • Where required and mutually agreed upon, TrialCore may enter into appropriate contractual arrangements, including Business Associate Agreements (BAAs), to support HIPAA-regulated use cases.
  • Customers remain responsible for determining whether HIPAA applies to their specific activities and ensuring appropriate organizational policies and procedures are followed.

Clinical Research Compliance

  • TrialCore is designed with awareness of common clinical research requirements and industry best practices, including Good Clinical Practice (GCP), ALCOA+ data integrity principles, FDA-regulated research workflows, sponsor oversight requirements, Institutional Review Board (IRB) documentation practices, and audit readiness expectations.
  • However, no software platform can independently guarantee regulatory compliance.
  • Compliance remains the responsibility of the study sponsor, investigator, institution, and authorized research personnel.

Data Integrity Principles

  • TrialCore is designed to support data integrity through user accountability, audit logging, timestamped activity records, change tracking, controlled access permissions, study-level data organization, and documentation of operational events.
  • These features are intended to support organizations implementing data integrity practices consistent with applicable regulations and research standards.

Security Measures

  • TrialCore is designed with security principles intended to help protect customer information and research data.
  • Security measures may include role-based access controls, authentication safeguards, encrypted network communications, secure cloud infrastructure, activity monitoring and logging, access management procedures, and routine security maintenance and updates.
  • Security controls may evolve over time as the platform matures and customer requirements expand.

Access Controls

  • Access to TrialCore is intended to be limited to authorized users.
  • Organizations may configure access according to operational responsibilities and study assignments.
  • Examples may include administrators, supervisors, study staff, and auditors.
  • Role-based permissions are intended to help ensure users access only the information necessary for their responsibilities.

Audit Trails and Accountability

  • TrialCore is designed to support operational transparency through audit logging.
  • Audit records may capture record creation, record updates, record deletion, user authentication events, data exports, study configuration changes, and workflow actions.
  • Audit functionality is intended to assist organizations with oversight, quality assurance, inspections, and internal review processes.

Data Retention

  • Research organizations are responsible for determining appropriate retention periods for their study data and documentation.
  • Retention requirements may vary based on study type, sponsor requirements, institutional policies, regulatory obligations, and contractual agreements.
  • Customers should establish retention practices appropriate to their regulatory environment.

Third-Party Services

  • TrialCore may utilize third-party infrastructure providers, hosting services, analytics tools, security services, or other vendors to support platform operations.
  • Such providers may process information only as necessary to perform contracted services and are expected to maintain appropriate security safeguards.

Incident Response

  • TrialCore takes security concerns seriously.
  • If a security incident affecting customer information is identified, TrialCore will take reasonable steps to investigate the incident, contain and remediate issues where possible, assess potential impact, and notify affected parties when required by applicable law or contractual obligations.

No Guarantee of Compliance

  • Although TrialCore is designed to support research operations and data management, TrialCore does not warrant or guarantee compliance with HIPAA, FDA regulations, ICH Good Clinical Practice (GCP), GDPR, state privacy laws, IRB requirements, sponsor requirements, or any other regulatory framework.
  • Organizations remain responsible for validating their processes, training personnel, maintaining documentation, and ensuring compliance with applicable requirements.

Future Enhancements

  • TrialCore continually evaluates opportunities to strengthen security, privacy, data governance, and compliance-related capabilities.
  • As the platform evolves, additional safeguards, certifications, and compliance features may be introduced to better support customer needs.

Contact Us

  • Questions regarding security, privacy, HIPAA considerations, or research data management may be directed to TrialCore.
  • Email: security@trialcore.com
  • Website: www.trialcore.com

TrialCore is committed to helping research teams manage study operations with transparency, accountability, and a focus on data integrity.
